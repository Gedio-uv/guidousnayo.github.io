Context

Spark started from a real situation: playing icebreaker card games in multilingual settings — language classes, expat groups, international friend circles. The games were good fun, but nothing combined genuine social play with structured language practice. So I started building it.

The core idea

The mechanic is deliberately simple: one device shared by a group, a question on screen, read aloud, and free conversation from there. At its base, it's a question game.

The twist is the entry choice. You can just play in your own language, or switch on learn a language mode and practise a target language through the same questions — turning a social game into low-pressure immersion.

Surface 01 · Session setup

Set the table before you play

Before a session you choose the language to learn, your level, the categories in play, and an optional timer. Level drives progressive immersion: Basic shows questions in your own language, Intermediate in the target language with a translation below, and Advanced in full immersion. Six categories carry 150 questions each, with a freemium split — General and Would You Rather are free, the rest unlock as premium packs.

Surface 02 · The card

One question at a time

In play, each card shows a single question — here in German with the English translation beneath — and you swipe for the next. The session timer runs as a dissolving glow around the screen rather than a hard countdown, and a quiet swipe-down marks a question as weak, so the content set sharpens over time. Category and level stay in view, with a simple 1 / 150 to track the deck.

Content & rules

  • Six categories, 150 questions each

    General, Would You Rather, Personal Growth, Kids & Family, Couples, and Spicy — multi-selectable for a single session.

  • A safety rule built into selection

    Kids & Family can't be combined with adult categories, so a family session can't surface content meant for a different room.

  • Freemium, not paywalled

    The free tier carries a full game; deeper themes are optional category packs at $1.99–$3.99.

How I built it

Full product ownership. Concept, UX architecture, user flow, visual identity, content strategy, and the monetization model are all mine. The whole product was shaped in conversation with Claude on claude.ai before a line of code, then built with Cursor and Claude as the coding agent.

Lo-fi on purpose. Phase 1 is a vanilla HTML, CSS, and JavaScript web app on GitHub Pages — chosen to validate the idea with zero setup before committing to native. Phase 3 targets iOS via React Native and Expo. The look leans premium and game-like — deep violet with pink accents, Syne for display — closer to a physical card game than a quiz app.
